Solutions 7.6

To find a fixed point, we solve (a + L ∘ λI)x = x. That is (I - λL)x = a with L ∈ O(2). But L can only have eigenvalues of ±1 and so the transformation I - λL is invertible unless λ = ±1 (when the transformation will be an isometry). Hence we can solve the equation to find a unique fixed point.
