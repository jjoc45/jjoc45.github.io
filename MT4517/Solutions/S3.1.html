Solution 3.1

The subring must contain 1, 1+1, ..., -1, -1 + (-1), ... and hence all the integers. Hence the subring is Z.
Since the sub-field must also contain all integers and their inverses and the products of these, it must contain all rational numbers and hence is Q.
In any commutative ring with an identity, the ideal < 1 > is the whole ring.
The subring of R generated by √2 contains all integer multiples of √2 as well as the integer 2. It is {a + b√2 | a ∈ 2Z, b ∈ Z }.
The subfield of R generated by √2 is {r + s√2 | r, s ∈ 2Q }.
