Solution 7.1

(i) Take the sequence (xn) = (1/n)→ 0. Then f (1/n) = 1. So, applying f to the sequence (xn) gives the constant sequence converging to 1 ≠ f (0). Hence f is not continuous at 0.

(ii) Take ε = 1/2. Then any interval (-δ, δ) containing 0 contains points where f (x) = 1 and so we cannot have |f (x) - f (0)| < ε for all the points in such an interval.
